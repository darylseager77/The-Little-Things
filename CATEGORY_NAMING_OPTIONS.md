# Category Naming - Finding the Right Words

## The Challenge

The original love languages are crystal clear:
- **Words of Affirmation** - you know exactly what this means
- **Quality Time** - obvious
- **Acts of Service** - clear
- **Receiving Gifts** - clear
- **Physical Touch** - clear (but needs work adaptation)

Our current workplace versions are too vague:
- "Connection" - connection to what? Could mean anything
- "Praise" - okay but corporate-y
- "Support" - too broad, generic HR-speak
- "Appreciation" - could mean all of the categories
- "Engagement" - vague corporate buzzword

**We need**: Clear, tangible, instantly understandable, workplace-appropriate names.

---

## Option A: Stay Closest to Original (Most Clear)

| Category | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Quality Time** | 1-on-1s, mentorship, deep conversations, being heard |
| **Words of Encouragement** | Verbal recognition, thank yous, acknowledgment, praise |
| **Acts of Service** | Helping with workload, removing blockers, practical support |
| **Gifts & Rewards** | Bonuses, time off, tangible perks, experiences |
| **Team Connection** | Collaboration, being included, team activities, belonging |

**Pros:**
- Clearest and most tangible
- People familiar with love languages will instantly get it
- "Quality Time" and "Acts of Service" are not trademarked as standalone phrases
- Easy to explain with examples

**Cons:**
- Might feel too "relationship therapy" for some workplaces
- "Acts of Service" might sound formal

---

## Option B: Workplace-Adapted (Professional but Clear)

| Category | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **One-on-One Time** | Regular check-ins, listening, mentorship, being heard |
| **Recognition** | Public/private acknowledgment, thank yous, praise |
| **Practical Help** | Support with workload, flexibility, removing obstacles |
| **Rewards & Perks** | Bonuses, gifts, time off, learning opportunities |
| **Inclusion** | Being invited, team activities, collaborative work |

**Pros:**
- Workplace-appropriate language
- Clear and specific
- Professional but not stuffy
- Easy for HR/leaders to adopt

**Cons:**
- "Practical Help" is a bit generic
- Loses some of the warmth of original framework

---

## Option C: Ultra Simple (One Word Each)

| Category | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Time** | Quality 1-on-1 time, listening, mentorship |
| **Words** | Verbal recognition, acknowledgment, encouragement |
| **Help** | Practical support, removing blockers, acts of service |
| **Rewards** | Tangible appreciation, gifts, bonuses, perks |
| **Belonging** | Team inclusion, collaboration, being part of things |

**Pros:**
- Extremely simple and memorable
- Visual/icon-friendly (can use symbols easily)
- Quick to say: "I'm a Time + Words person"
- Not corporate jargon

**Cons:**
- Might be TOO simple - need examples to clarify
- "Help" might feel needy
- Less distinctive

---

## Option D: Employee-Centric (From Their POV)

| Category | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Listen to Me** | Give me quality time, hear me out, 1-on-1s |
| **Acknowledge Me** | Recognize my work, thank me, tell me I'm doing well |
| **Support Me** | Help with my workload, give me flexibility, remove blockers |
| **Reward Me** | Give me tangible appreciation, bonuses, perks, time off |
| **Include Me** | Invite me, collaborate with me, make me part of the team |

**Pros:**
- Makes it about the employee's needs
- Very clear what each means
- Empowering language
- Memorable

**Cons:**
- "Me Me Me" might feel entitled to some
- Longer phrases
- Might not work well for leader-facing materials

---

## Option E: Action-Oriented (What Leaders Do)

| Category | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Spend Time** | Regular 1-on-1s, listening, being present |
| **Say Thanks** | Verbal recognition, acknowledgment, appreciation |
| **Lend a Hand** | Help with workload, provide support, remove obstacles |
| **Give Rewards** | Tangible recognition, gifts, bonuses, perks |
| **Build Together** | Team activities, collaboration, inclusion |

**Pros:**
- Action verbs make it clear what to DO
- Leader-friendly language
- Warm but professional
- Specific actions, not abstract concepts

**Cons:**
- Longer phrases
- Might lose some clarity

## Testing the Names

Here's how I'd test these with real people in your interviews:

**Test 1: Instant Understanding**
"When I say [category name], what comes to mind?"
- If they get it immediately ‚Üí good name
- If they're confused ‚Üí bad name

**Test 2: Personal Resonance**
"Which of these 5 categories matters most to you at work?"
- If they can easily choose ‚Üí good names
- If they need lots of explanation ‚Üí bad names

**Test 3: Examples Match**
Show them examples and ask: "Which category is this?"
- If they match correctly ‚Üí good names
- If they're confused ‚Üí need clearer names
