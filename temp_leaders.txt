Step-by-Step Guide
1. Setting the Context
Before using the cards, set a clear intention. Decide whether the focus will be on improving team dynamics, enhancing individual performance, or addressing specific workplace challenges. Communicate this intention to your team to ensure everyone is aligned.
2. Choosing the Right Cards
	•	For Leaders: Select cards that align with the needs of your team or individual team members. For example, if the goal is to foster better communication, choose cards from the "Words of Affirmation" category.
	•	For Teams: If working with a group, let team members pick cards that resonate with them or that they believe will benefit the team.
	•	For Self-Reflection: Employees can choose cards that reflect their personal preferences or areas they want to improve or focus on.
3. Discussing and Aligning
	•	Team Meetings: Use the cards in team meetings to initiate discussions. Each team member can pick a card and share how they think the action could be implemented.
	•	One-on-One Sessions: During individual check-ins, use the cards to explore areas where the employee feels most and least engaged. Discuss how the selected card’s action can be applied.
	•	Workshops or Retreats: Utilize the cards in group activities or workshops to brainstorm ideas for improving team culture or to develop action plans.
4. Implementing the Actions
Once the cards are chosen, create a plan to implement the actions:
	•	Assign Responsibilities: Clearly define who will be responsible for each action. This could be a leader, a team member, or a group.
	•	Set Timelines: Establish a timeline for when each action should be implemented and evaluated.
	•	Monitor Progress: Regularly check in on the progress of the actions. Use subsequent meetings to discuss what’s working and what may need adjustment.


5. Reflecting and Iterating
	•	Reflection Sessions: After the actions have been implemented, hold a session to reflect on their impact. Did the actions improve engagement? What changes were observed?
	•	Feedback Loop: Encourage continuous feedback from team members. Adjust and iterate on the chosen actions based on this feedback.
	•	Repeat the Process: Periodically revisit the cards to address new challenges or to reinforce positive behaviors and practices.
6. Celebrating Successes
	•	Recognition: When a card’s action leads to a positive outcome, celebrate it! Use the "Words of Affirmation" or "Gifts" cards to recognize and reward the efforts.
	•	Documentation: Keep a record of the actions that worked well. This documentation can serve as a reference for future engagement strategies.


























10 Questions for Consistent Check-ins 

How have you been feeling about your work recently?
What’s something you’ve accomplished recently that you’re proud of?
Are there any challenges you’re currently facing that we could work on together?
How do you feel about the support you’re receiving? Is there anything more you need?
What’s one thing that could make your experience at work better?
Do you feel you’re receiving enough feedback on your work?
Is there a skill or area you’re interested in developing further?
How do you feel about our team’s communication and collaboration?
Is there anything I can do to support you better?
Do you feel recognized and valued for your contributions?
